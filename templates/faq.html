Security

Is my data safe and secure?

Absolutely. All files are processed using secure, encrypted connections (SSL/TLS). Your uploaded documents are only accessible during processing and are automatically deleted after one hour. We do not store, share, or access your document contents.

How long do you keep my files?

All uploaded and converted files are automatically deleted from our servers after 1 hour. This ensures your privacy and keeps our systems running efficiently. We recommend downloading your converted file immediately after processing.

Can you convert password-protected PDFs?

Currently, FlipDoc cannot process password-protected or encrypted PDF files. You will need to remove the password protection from your PDF before uploading it for conversion. This is a security measure to protect document ownership.

Is my connection encrypted during upload?

Yes, all file uploads and downloads use SSL/TLS encryption (HTTPS). This ensures your data is protected during transit between your device and our servers. We follow industry-standard security practices for all data transfers.

File Limits

What is the maximum file size I can upload?

The maximum file size is 20MB. This limit ensures fast processing and optimal performance for all users. If your PDF is larger, consider splitting it into smaller sections or compressing it before uploading.

How many pages can a PDF have?

Up to 100 pages per document. For larger documents, split into multiple PDFs and convert them separately. Most documents convert well within a single pass up to this limit.

What should I do if my PDF won't convert?

First, check that your PDF is not corrupted, under 20MB, and not password-protected. Refresh the page and try uploading again. If the issue persists, try converting individual pages or check that the file is a valid PDF (not just renamed).

Accuracy

How accurate is the OCR?

Our PaddleOCR engine achieves 95%+ accuracy on clear, well-scanned documents. Accuracy depends on the quality of your source PDF — good contrast, straight pages, and readable fonts produce the best results. Handwritten text is not well supported at this time.

Are images and formatting preserved?

Yes, FlipDoc preserves images, charts, graphics, tables, and basic formatting during conversion. We maintain document structure, font sizes, and layout as much as possible. Very complex layouts may require minor adjustments after conversion.

What types of PDFs work best?

Text-based PDFs with clear fonts and simple layouts produce the most accurate conversions. Scanned documents with good contrast and resolution also work very well. For best results, ensure your PDF is not password-protected and is under 20MB.

Formats

What format will I get after conversion?

The output file is in Microsoft Word format (.docx). This format is compatible with Microsoft Word, Google Docs, LibreOffice Writer, Apple Pages, and most other word processing applications. You can edit it immediately after download.

Can I use FlipDoc on my mobile device?

Yes. FlipDoc is fully responsive and works on smartphones and tablets. The interface adapts to your screen size. No app download is needed — just visit the website on any device with a browser.

Do you support other output formats?

Currently we output only .docx (Microsoft Word) format. DOCX is the most widely compatible editable document format. Additional output formats may be added in the future based on user demand.

Pricing

Is FlipDoc completely free to use?

Yes, FlipDoc is completely free with no hidden charges, subscription fees, or premium tiers. You can convert as many PDF files as you need without any cost. We believe document conversion should be accessible to everyone.

Do I need to register or create an account?

No registration is required. You can start converting PDF files immediately without creating an account or providing any personal information. This ensures your privacy and makes the process quick and hassle-free.

How long does the conversion process take?

Most PDF files are converted within 15-60 seconds, depending on file size and complexity. Simple text-based PDFs convert faster, while scanned documents requiring OCR processing may take a bit longer. You can track progress in real-time on the conversion page.
